Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

Timeline

  • 1941 - Born in Bronxville, New York.
  • 1967 - Begins work at Bell Labs.
  • 1968 - Defends his PhD Thesis, "Program Structure and Computational Complexity." 2
  • 1970s - Continues work on UNIX and other programs at Bell Labs.
  • 1980s: Works begins on Plan 9, a distributed OS.
  • 1990s: Plan 9 is retired and work begins on Inferno.
  • 1996: Ritchie releases information about the new Inferno OS, essentially leaking its existence after a year of development.
  • 2007: Retires from Lucent Technologies (Formerly, Bell Labs).
  • 2011: Died in Berkeley Heights, New Jersey. (Age: 70)

Quotes

UNIX is basically a simple operating system, but you have to be a genius to understand the simplicity.

A language that doesn't have everything is actually easier to program in than some that do.

When I read commentary about suggestions for where C should go, I often think back and give thanks that it wasn't developed under the advice of a worldwide crowd.
